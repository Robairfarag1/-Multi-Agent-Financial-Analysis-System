Glossary

  • EPS (Earnings Per Share): A company's profit divided by the outstanding shares of its common stock.
  • Earnings Surprise: The difference between the actual earnings and the expected earnings, often expressed as a percentage.
  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings Ratio): A valuation ratio calculated by dividing the current share price by the earnings per share.
  • Market Capitalization: The total market value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated by multiplying the share price by the total number of shares.
  • Volatility: A statistical measure of the dispersion of returns for a given security or market index, often associated with the level of risk.
